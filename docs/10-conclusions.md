# **Conclusions**
## **Conclusions and recommendations.**
The development of the project represented a significant technical challenge, as it required the integration of multiple concepts such as user-centered design, agile methodologies, and frontend implementation. Even though the scope of this stage was focused on the Landing Page, the team had to translate abstract requirements and user stories into a functional and visually coherent product. This process demanded not only technical skills in HTML, CSS, and responsive design, but also the ability to structure information effectively to communicate the value proposition of the system.

From a process perspective, the use of Scrum and iterative development through Sprints proved to be essential for organizing the workflow and ensuring continuous progress. Defining Sprint Goals, managing the Product Backlog, and applying version control practices such as GitFlow and Conventional Commits allowed the team to maintain order, traceability, and consistency throughout the development. These practices also facilitated incremental improvements and early validation of deliverables, reducing risks and improving overall product quality.

On a collaborative level, the project highlighted the importance of effective communication and clear role distribution within the team. Coordinating tasks, aligning ideas, and resolving challenges required constant interaction among team members. As the project progressed, the team improved its ability to collaborate, make decisions, and support each other, which was reflected in better organization and efficiency. This experience reinforced that, beyond technical knowledge, teamwork and communication are critical factors for the success of any software development project.

For future iterations of the project, it is recommended to deepen the technical implementation by integrating the Landing Page with backend services and real-time functionalities. This would allow validating the complete system architecture, including RESTful APIs, authentication mechanisms, and data synchronization processes. Additionally, incorporating usability testing with real users at earlier stages would provide valuable feedback to refine both the interface and the overall user experience. Strengthening testing practices, such as automated testing and performance evaluation, would also improve the reliability and scalability of the solution.

From a teamwork perspective, it is advisable to continue reinforcing structured communication and task management practices. While the team showed improvement over time, establishing more frequent checkpoints and clearer documentation of decisions could further enhance coordination and reduce potential misunderstandings. Moreover, promoting a more proactive distribution of responsibilities and encouraging all members to participate in both technical and design discussions would lead to a more balanced contribution and a stronger collective understanding of the project.
